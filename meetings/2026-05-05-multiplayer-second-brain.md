## Key themes

### Second brain as a coaching tool

Matt's primary use case for multiplayer AI: **surface specific examples of project clarity (or lack of it)** to use in 1:1 coaching conversations. Not to write to Linear or manage work — to prepare for conversations with his team.

- PM team producing AI-generated projects with 3,000 words each — "they've not read it, so I'm definitely not reading it"
- Current state: Matt scans through projects manually to find things to raise in 1:1s — time-consuming and ad hoc
- Desired state: second brain reads all team projects, pulls out "so what" moments, gives Matt concrete examples to work with

### PM level-up challenge

- Team wants promotions and seniority — but doing more of the same at the same level won't get them there
- Matt wants to coach them toward **business-level thinking** vs team-level thinking
- Problem: hard to coach them to write "what does success look like for Anton reading this?" when SLT priorities and strategies aren't clearly codified
- Geran is further along than Ollie here — has experience to draw from. Ollie has had "bring me a rock" problem — given freedom without enough structure for what good looks like

### Ollie doom loop — Anton/Darren conflict

- Anton told Ollie: don't do the pricing stuff, do other work with Adam instead
- Darren told Ollie: Anton is wrong, you should be doing this direction instead
- Matt trying to help Ollie navigate — but doesn't think Ollie should be expected to manage two differing SLT opinions alone
- Matt's framing: stop trying to reconcile SLT positions, instead build a data-led proposal that is compelling enough to force alignment

### SLT multiplayer — the holy grail, not the first step

- Real goal: SLT-level shared brain — answering "why is X in now vs next vs later?"
- Current reality: Matt doesn't have a clear answer to that question even from inside the product team
- Both agreed: can't jump straight to SLT — need to demonstrate the process (messily) at a smaller team level first
- Expect both the process and outcome to be wrong the first couple of times
- Proposed first step: collaboratively backfill the product team's now/next/later with the PMs, as a way of both creating the context and teaching the team what "good" looks like

### Linear as the face, not the context

- Linear shows the decided outcome — the context behind that (the "why") lives in Notion or wherever
- Initiatives in Linear don't have enough supporting context for a second brain to understand the *why*
- This is the structural problem: can't "roll up" to a meaningful strategy when the foundations aren't there

### Dotwork

- Met with Dotwork (John Cutler's company) — AI strategy tool for companies, starting at $75k/year
- Too heavy, too process-dependent, too expensive for Flock right now
- Interesting inspiration for outcome-focused north stars that persist beyond a quarter
