## Context

First discovery conversation with Adam (Head of Distribution). Distribution flagged by Fergus as fertile ground. Adam's team already using AI daily — this was about understanding what they do, where they are, and what they need.

## Notes

### Distribution team overview

- ~300 brokers, some with 50+ UK offices — contact points easily double that
- High-volume, high-touch: growing the panel, trading data, review packs, broker account management
- Team: Sophie Dodds, Alex Dybell, new hire coming in, plus Adam himself

### Current AI usage

- **Granola on every meeting** — shared folder for all broker meetings
  - Can interrogate across all notes: "which brokers mentioned haulage or horse insurance?"
  - Accidental CRM — solves the 40-year problem of salespeople not filling in Salesforce
- **ChatGPT/Claude** for interrogating data sets
- **Kirsty's Looker MCP integration** — trading data queryable via AI
  - "Look at Sophie's broker panel, find where performance changed, identify brokers to review"
  - Can auto-generate review packs from trading data
  - Kirsty has presented this at AI breakfasts
- Team proficiency: Matt and Ollie Crowe are advanced; Sophie, Alex, and new hire need structured tools

### Adam's philosophy: "selfish with AI"

- Wants the team to use AI as an efficiency tool, not spend time building models
- Doesn't want Sophie or Alex losing half a day building a custom GPT
- Preferred model: define a **toolkit** of high-impact ready-made tools, give them to the team, let them execute
  - Example: "before your broker review meeting, don't build a slide deck — use this skill, plug it in, go"
- Fundamentals first (slide production, broker prioritisation, data interrogation), then free exploration on top
- Distinct from how you'd work with Matt/Ollie — they need freedom, not frameworks

### Strategic themes

- **Granola as knowledge base** — distribution team now has a genuine queryable record of all broker conversations
- **HubSpot is thin** on broker account data (fleet size, sector specialisms, premium value)
  - Adam and Emily have a joint project to clean up HubSpot structure
  - Ideal pipeline: Granola → HubSpot → Claude via MCP — meeting data flows into CRM and becomes queryable
- **Semantic/naming consistency** — systems need to recognise the same broker across tools (prerequisite for cross-system querying)
- **Hypothesis validation** — can Claude objectively confirm or challenge assumptions about which broker types perform best? Remove bias from account prioritisation
- **Company-wide data pools** — resonates with insight layer thinking: emails, Granola notes, telemetry, HubSpot, PDFs — how do we create query pathways for all of them?

## Decisions

- Adam's team needs a ready-made toolkit, not a "go explore AI" mandate
- Tom to act as bridge between advanced users (Matt, Ollie) and broader distribution team (Sophie, Alex, new hire)

## Actions

- [ ] Tom: work with Adam to define a distribution-specific AI toolkit — ready-made skills for slide decks, broker prioritisation, data interrogation
- [ ] Adam: book team session with Kirsty to integrate with Looker MCP / review pack workflow
- [ ] Adam + Emily: continue HubSpot clean-up project to enable richer broker data
- [ ] Tom: think strategically about company-wide data pools and query pathways (Granola, HubSpot, emails — links to [[insight-layer]])
