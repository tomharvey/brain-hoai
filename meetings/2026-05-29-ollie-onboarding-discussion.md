## Key themes

### Finance hackathon structure — peer-to-peer role interviews
- Proposal: pair up finance team members to describe their role to each other via Granola recording
- Transcript feeds into Claude to generate automation ideas and build understanding of the role's objectives
- Benefits over self-serve approach: more information extracted, dynamic follow-up questions, easier than building a coaching bot
- Key design principle: make sure interview covers "why you do your job" (outcomes), not just "how you do it" (process)
- Less experienced staff tend to describe the process; more experienced understand the objective — the interview design should bridge this gap

### Implementation design
- Peer uses an interview sheet (structured questions) to ensure depth
- Claude prompt at the end checks for completeness and asks follow-up questions
- Could also let Claude ask dynamic follow-ups based on earlier answers
- Previous hackathon model: team goes to a room, speaks to Granola, then builds
- Francesco (Venerandi) has Pace onboarding materials that may have similar structure — worth reviewing

### AI breakfast insight from Jake Wood
- Jake (underwriting) actively inputting at AI breakfast; wants to roll out tools to his team
- Tom meeting Jake Monday to see the dashboard
- Jake's pattern: self-starter who sees AI as personal competitive advantage
- Challenge: getting other team members to adopt once Jake's been the pioneer

### Use case clarity as adoption driver
- People starting to see AI as 10x multiplier for their role
- Shift from "mechanical automation" thinking to "thinking enhancement"
- Process vs. outcome understanding varies by experience level
