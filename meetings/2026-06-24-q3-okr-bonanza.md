## Key themes

### Jade (OKR 1) — full RFI book rollout

- Q2 pilot: 23 private customers; Q3 objective is full book rollout
- Key results:
  - 100% of Flock customers get access
  - 50% activation (at least one session)
  - 20% return rate among activated customers
- Tactics: formalise pilot feedback loop (target 50% response rate), red-team emails and outputs before launch, internal training embedded into sales workflow
- In-person onboarding flagged as likely necessary for meaningful activation — Paul's team already running AI-focused safety review sessions with customers
- Measuring downstream benefit is a chicken-and-egg problem: no baseline yet for safety actions taken from Jade insights
- Jade already a strong conversion tool in demos — risk noted: Tom Rogers told a haulage customer Jade "will" support per-depot reporting (not confirmed feature)
- Broker version explicitly out of scope for Q3; noted as future direction
- Resource constraint: connectivity work from haulage OKR involves Geran from territory sectors

### Finance (OKR 3) — match the market

- Current state: >50% of invoices to brokers and customers edited or created by hand; ~30 broker disputes/month
- Q3 focus:
  - Launch installments with no restrictions (all customers, all edge cases: cancellations, mid-policy changes, rebates)
  - Built on Mods + potential Stripe partnership
  - Tailored broker deals: portfolio-level now, deal-specific in Q4
- What good looks like by end Q3:
  - Zero invoices or transactions created by hand on the new platform
  - 75%+ of all invoices generated automatically across both platforms
  - Broker-disputed invoices down from 30/month to zero
- Risks raised: current automation covers only ~30–40% of cases; full automation is multi-quarter
- Realistic estimate: ~30% reduction in manual transactions by end Q3, not zero
- Upfront broker commission and fees deferred to Q4
