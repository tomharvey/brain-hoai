## Context

Sam presented his full operational model for an AI Community of Practice (CoP). This was a follow-up to earlier conversations where Sam proposed creating structure around AI adoption company-wide.

## Key points

### Sam's CoP proposal

- **Operational model**: strategy roadmap with compliance integration, initiative tracking system, filterable dashboard by department
- **Four delivery streams**: Workshops, Processes, Products, Policy
- **8-week delivery cycles** with monthly/weekly standard meetings
- **Initiative board**: used Claude to scrape Notion for all existing AI initiatives — consolidated list so people can find existing work and collaborate rather than duplicate
- **Roles**: Tom as sponsor, cross-org participants selected by delivery need, open participation for interested contributors
- **Standard deliverables**: use case papers, value documentation, evaluation frameworks for every initiative

### Pushback

- **Tom**: ambitious — "might take 8 weeks just to take everyone through it". Company doesn't respond well to "big hit" approaches. Valid ideas but trying all at once sets us up for failure.
- **Jordi**: agrees it's the 100% vision but need to start small. Beachhead strategy — show value with one thing, attract interest, then expand. Everyone has day jobs.
- **Mima** (referenced): "we're already doing this" — initiatives moved fast while Sam was still planning

### What was agreed

- Sam to **focus on a single initiative**: documentation pipeline (aligned to his Q2 goal)
- Remove other initiatives from board, start with documentation, show value first
- Involve others — not a single-person effort. Chris and Javi identified for eval/docs content
- Present documentation work at Lunch & Learn or AI Breakfast to demonstrate value
- Thursday 1:1 (Sam/Jordi) to set deadlines, identify collaborators, plan execution

## Key quotes

- Tom: "I think as a company we're very bad at or we just don't respond well to taking a sort of big hit... that beachhead strategy is going to land easier than trying to come in with a four piece framework"
- Tom: "I don't disagree with any of these things... I think to try and get on top of all of them in one goal would set us up for failure"
- Jordi: "Start showing a small thing and then people are going to ask 'oh my god I'm interested in this, can you explain me how you did this'"
