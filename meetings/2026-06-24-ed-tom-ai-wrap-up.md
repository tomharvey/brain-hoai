## Key themes

### Q2 activation — strong progress, right framing

- Tom's framework puts ~90% of company at activation level (using AI + bringing in data)
- ~75% building their own tools — target 80%+
- ~50% at the top end: handing off whole tasks and reviewing outputs
- Finance and underwriting flagged as the priority teams for Q3 — both have activated individuals but haven't moved as teams
- UWA assistants now need finance and underwriting to change workflows to feed into the system
- [[jake-wood]]'s underwriting dashboard called out by Ed as one of the best internal AI projects — practical, not fancy, actually used
- [[tom-rogers]] is doing more than he gets credit for — using AI as a research assistant on data

### Q3 framing — production systems, not experimentation

- Q2 was the experiment-and-activate phase; Q3 is about production-grade systems
- People are making the right mistakes now (Adam with bad data, duplication of effort) — this means engagement is real; next step is tooling and governance to do it right
- Ed's framing: intensity needs to increase significantly; 90 minutes a quarter is not enough

### Company-wide benchmarking survey

- Ed has committed to Admiral Pioneer that Flock will run a company-wide AI benchmarking exercise
- Tom to work with [[rakhee]] on framework + survey — 60-second, dogfood with AI, present results with AI-generated visualisation
- Aim: validate/invalidate finance and underwriting hunch, give Admiral a presentable output
- Tension on rubric: Flock-specific framework can't be benchmarked externally; Zapier CEO's 1–5 framework mentioned as an alternative
- Ed: keep it simple, don't over-engineer — a messy benchmark today beats a perfect one in six months
- Tom and [[rakhee]] to present to Ed in London (~Jul 8)

### Framework into hiring, performance, and promotions

- All new hires to be assessed against the framework from now on
- Engineers not using Claude or Cursor flagged as a red line
- Framework to inform exits for non-progressors and promotions for leaders
- [[mima]] (Jemima Pitceathly) cited by Ed as someone pushing the boundary who deserves recognition

### AI partnering team — Q3 pilot

- Proposal: deputize a small AI team as a ghostbusters capability unit — anyone can bring a ticket, get it built in 24 hours
- Named candidates: [[jordi]], [[mima]], [[jake-wood]]
- Test for Q3; keep it light and informal
- Broader model: PM defines what to build → AI mechanics (finance, ops staff) build → architects ([[jordi]], Chris, Tom) handle systems thinking → [[geran]] handles product thinking
- Finance and ops people as AI mechanics, not architects — smaller ask, faster unlock
- PMs to have 4–5 extra pairs of hands in Q3 via this model

- Most companies focus on process automation; Flock's angle is how AI changes how you manage people relationships
- Ed has limited visibility into this — opportunity to brief him properly
