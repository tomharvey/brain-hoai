## Key themes

### Strategic shift: TCO value docs
- Previously producing value/TCO documents for all deals meeting a threshold (£100k+)
- Strategic change led by Adam and Tom: now produced **on request only for deals >£500k**
- Approach has shifted from comprehensive docs to key messages at each deal stage
- All tokens previously spent on this automation have stopped. High quality outputs, but over-resourced for every deal.

### Enterprise prospecting: email automation abandoned → phone calls
- The 9-agent email pipeline did not move the needle on response rates: **single-digit percentages** despite a compelling Admiral acquisition story
- Cold email is fundamentally limited. Matt switched back to **phone calls as primary outreach**
- AI now acts as **"chief of staff"** — targeted research on specific businesses rather than automation at scale
- **600 target companies** now in scope (was 64 a month ago). Pre-built target list, refreshed periodically rather than continuously expanded

### Prioritisation criteria for the 600
- Renewal timing and buying signals (especially telematics investments)
- Fleet size sweet spot: **500–2,000 vehicles** (below mega-fleets that self-insure, above where Flock becomes viable)
- Market experience informing which accounts to cherry-pick for deep research

### Claude Co-Work as chief of staff
- Full role context loaded into a Co-Work project
- Daily briefs, task prioritisation, accountability coaching ("be ruthless with me — if I'm dodging a diary block, call it out")
- Briefly switched back to ChatGPT for its preset agents; returned to Claude for integrations
- Matt: "it's almost like a direct line manager that's totally associated to me"

### £2M deal: AI as analytical partner
- Complex deal with multiple excess levels and connectivity/telematics requirements
- Claude built a layman's model of various excess options — would have taken hours manually
- Used to **challenge underwriting and pricing** from an informed position. "I wouldn't have been able to do that before"
- Demonstrates AI as strategic partner, not just execution tool
