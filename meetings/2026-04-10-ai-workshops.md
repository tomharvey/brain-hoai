## Key decisions

### Strategy: confidence before automation
Strong consensus that the priority right now is **building confidence**, not automating the right things.

- Automating the "right" things will naturally filter into prioritisation frameworks later
- Right now, if someone cares about automating something and it teaches them to fish — that's the win
- Kirsty's dashboard example: the "cool factor" has inherent value right now because it drives engagement, even if the insight layer needs work
- The outcome for this group = getting people to feel like Kirsty: confident, showing their work, seeing AI make their job easier

Quote from Fergus: *"The stuff that is objectively broken is more likely to make its way onto a roadmap somewhere."* This group focuses on capability building, not roadmap items.

### Approach: three layers
1. **Company-wide workshop** — Oli to lead a session on Claude Co-Work and MCP connectors (replacing manual Zapier workflows). Potential slot at April 23rd town hall.
2. **Department workshops** — two-part process: discovery session → digest → implementation/training. Finance identified as good first candidate (cancellations, Slack message processing). People team volunteered as early adopter.
3. **1:1 pairing** — Tom continues individual sessions. More effective for confidence building than group workshops for people who are unsure/hesitant.

### Teach a person to fish
- [[fergus]] preference: individuals learn to do things themselves wherever possible
- This group focuses on things that require organisational-level solutions (e.g. productionising skills, deploying MCP servers)
- Unreasonable to expect teams to productionise skills at org level; reasonable to expect an underwriter to refine a prompt for their daily task
