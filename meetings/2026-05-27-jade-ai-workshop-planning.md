## Key themes

### Workshop design challenge
- Jade planning an AI workshop for the finance team
- Core tension: half the team would thrive with autonomy; the other half (more junior) struggle to identify what the outcomes of their job even are
- Tom's reframe: even if people don't build AI tooling, understanding their job objectives is "fundamentally a better position for the company to be in" — this is a useful output regardless

### Core exercise — adopted from Tom's suggestion
Jade scrapped her original idea and adopted this framework:

1. **Start with the job, not the tool**: participants write down what they'd normally be working on today. What are the objectives/outcomes of their role?
2. **Use Granola + Claude**: record a conversation with Claude (or Jade, or Christian) about what their job is and what they're trying to achieve. Feed that transcript to Claude.
3. **Ask**: how can AI help with this process?
4. Outcome: some will redesign a process entirely (cancellations out of Google Sheets, through Claude); others will simply set up a prompt to automate their monthly pack. Both are wins.

**Hackathon precedent**: a team at the tech AI hackathon went into a room, talked about what they wanted to build while Granola recorded, then fed it to Claude and said "build it." No typing, just talking.

### Structure — individual work + roaming tutors
- Pairing rejected: stronger participants tend to take over, not help
- Individual work preferred — but Jade didn't want "three hours of people typing silently"
- **Roaming tutors model**: pull out experienced people (Jade has already identified candidates) to support others
- Tom suggested Jade make this an odd-number-friendly format — one person pulled as tutor for each group

### Shreya (underwriting assistants) as tutor / pairing partner
- Shreya (from Emily's underwriting assistant team) has reworked processes, understands boundary-team blocking issues
- At the point where the parts that feed into her process **from finance** are blocking her
- Tom suggested: ask Emily if Shreya can spend an hour in the workshop as a roaming tutor — or commit to pairing Shreya with a finance team member on the **cancellations process**
- Outcome: finance team member gets help; Shreya's cross-team friction is addressed; both teams benefit
- Cancellations process is broken partly because the platform can't handle it — but workflow improvements can be made in the meantime
