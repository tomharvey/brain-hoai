## What Dotwork is

- Knowledge graph platform for aligning strategy, delivery, and outcomes across orgs
- AI-forward: designed for a world where humans and agents work side by side toward shared goals
- Not a UI-first product — core value is the **context layer and the graph**, consumable via MCP (e.g. through Claude)
- Integrates with Jira, Linear, Google Sheets, and others; pulls data into a living, configurable graph
- Onboarding is white-glove: map current operating model, define key questions per team/leader, populate context
- Team background: ex-Atlassian (Jira Align / Agile Craft) + ex-PlanView. 20-30 years of portfolio/strategy management expertise baked into the graph model
- Barry O'Reilly (wrote "Artificial Organizations") is an advisor

## Key themes

### Shared context is the core unsolved problem

- Tom and Jemima both articulated the single-player → multiplayer gap
- Personal OSes (markdown files in Claude/Cursor) are effective individually but not shared
- Some things Tom wouldn't want to share (opinions), some that should be shared — need selective visibility

### Current Flock AI stack

- Claude (widely adopted), Granola, Lovable, Notion, Linear, HubSpot
- Moving away from ChatGPT naturally, not enforced
- Notion becoming less useful — "less used than a bunch of markdown files"

### Where Flock is on the journey

- Right now: execution and planning. Strategy layer is next.
- Q3/Q4 goal: move from capability uplift to measuring whether AI bets are driving the right outcomes
- Early product signals: 150% engagement increase on some pages, reduced cycle times via Lovable + Granola prototyping
- No formal financial ROI measurement yet

### Business process documentation angle

- "If you can teach an LLM to do it, you've finally written down your business processes"
- Flock using operational efficiency initiatives to flush out actual business processes
- Goal: query how the business actually works, not how we think it works

### Dotwork's moat

- Not the UI — the 20-30 years of portfolio/strategy management expertise encoded in the graph model
- Configurable, living graph (not locked into day-one decisions like Jira Align)
- Snapshots over time — how you thought about things historically
