## Key themes

### Trading review project — the flagship output
- Built a **Claude Chat project for the SLT and trading team**: single source of truth consolidating multiple data sources. Shared with Kirsty as data co-owner — she'll verify data sources when back from holiday.
- Before: screenshots of dashboards, manual red/amber/green dots, manually typed numbers vs targets. Now: structured monthly trading pack with layers:
  - Executive summary: targets vs performance
  - Pipeline submission values by segment
  - Conversion rates and new business output
  - Monthly split: wholesale vs retail brokers
  - Renewal tracking: upcoming + recently lost
  - Top 10 retail broker performance
  - Lost reasons and who to
  - 30-day pipeline forecast
- Quality: "amazing" — Adam shared it with people and they said "unbelievable." Tom's challenge: "What stops you making this without AI?" Answer: "Three junior members of staff" and weeks of time.
- Tom's push: "Why is this report better?" Adam's answer: layers — executive simplicity plus ability to drill into any segment. It builds the layers up well.
- Quality control: a couple of rounds of reteaching needed during build; now "signed off." Next run will be the real test — updated data, same format.
- Note: **needs pack consistency follow-up** — quality has occasionally varied between runs.

### Co-Work vs Chat Projects
- Adam built this in Chat projects (not Co-Work). Used Claude itself to help design the project structure.
- Tom clarified: Co-Work is better when you have a folder of files (Excel, PDFs). For structured data + recurring queries, Chat projects are probably right.
- Kirsty as data owner is the right governance call — she owns what gets pulled from where.

### AI as strategic partner — beyond the pack
- Separate from the trading pack, Adam is using Claude for **strategic thinking**: feeding in Granola transcripts for leadership coaching, processing 1:1 feedback, preparing for complex stakeholder meetings.
- Admiral paper example: three-stakeholder navigation (internal Flock, personal objectives, Admiral requirements). Fed in Granola transcripts + objectives + the paper → AI helped shape thinking and challenged direction.
- Recorded a conversation with his father (former haulage broker) on Granola and fed it into the same thread → AI verified the paper against that conversation.
- Tom observed: "You're using it as a council."
- **Key pattern emerging:** *Secretary → Coach → Strategic Partner*. Adam is at strategic partner level; most of the team are at secretary or coach. Tom flagging this pattern to [[rakhee]].

### Matt Lees — Adam's read
- Matt "has a tendency to start with the most complicated, scalable solution possible" — built wonderful AI tooling that was "a complete waste of time with what we're trying to task him to do"
- Adam told him directly: start basic. His role is speaking to a handful of high-value customers, not managing 1,000 contacts.
- Tom confirmed: Matt acknowledged he'd spent more time on tooling than his job. Matt has walked it back.
- Consistent: Adam sees Matt as someone who needs guardrails on over-engineering, not capability uplift.

### Team philosophy: "be incredibly selfish with AI"
- Adam's direct message to his team: be selfish — ask what you want from it, don't explore for exploration's sake while learning is happening
- His question to Tom: how do we **build structures for the team** rather than asking each person to build their own? Consistency + efficiency argument.
- Tom: the balance is helping them understand the tool vs handing it to them. "You have to learn to fish on some of these." But there's a role for providing the structure once individuals have found their wedge.

### Activation observation
