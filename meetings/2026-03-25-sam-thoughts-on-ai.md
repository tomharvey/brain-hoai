# Sam's thoughts on AI

**Date**: 2026-03-25 10:16
**Attendees**: [[sam]], Tom

## Key themes

### AI adoption speed vs guardrails
- Sam's analogy: "riding a power bike at 200 mph as an amateur" — good adoption speed but no guidance structure
- Need systematic approach to balance innovation with risk management

### Risk scenarios identified
1. Agent accidentally deletes all Looker data
2. Malicious prompting exploits (reference: someone got 80% discount through persistent prompting)
3. Data exposure — more concerned about data ending up "on Reddit" than being lost

### AI guidelines framework needed
- Company-wide guidelines, not just implementation focus
- Should cover risk awareness, prevention strategies, response protocols across all departments
- Current prompting workshop was beginner-level only

### Prompt engineering risks
- "Evil prompts" and manipulation vulnerabilities
- Agents easily led by question framing
- Risk of hallucination when data unavailable
- Potential for agents to adjust safety scores inappropriately
