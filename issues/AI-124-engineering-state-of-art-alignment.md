# AI-124 — Design approach to align engineering team around AI state of the art

Fergus's direct offer: "Aligning around the state of the art. And helping the team that isn't there yet understand that there is a route through to what we're describing. Is something I think we should do. I don't know how to. That's an offer to you."

Two interleaved problems:
1. **Scope discipline** — submissions pipeline took ~1 year with no version-zero shipped. This is a product/cultural problem, not an AI problem.
2. **The evolutionary leap** — from "I use AI to help me write code" to "the AI IS the code." Tom is there; no one else on the team is yet.

Constraints:
- Retro on submissions pipeline won't yield a constructive conversation
- Greenfield hackathons don't transfer to legacy constraints (proven in last hackathon)
- G-forces are real — people will tap out if pushed too fast
- Baby steps work: Jordi's quality/alert work got real engagement; Chris writing 100x more tests

Possible approaches discussed:
- Selective seeding hackathon (Fergus, Tom, Jordi, Rob) with a specific goal
- Continue the quality/testing on-ramp (extend Jordi's success)
- Workshop with legacy codebase, not greenfield

Open question: are the scope discipline problem and the AI evolutionary leap the same problem or different problems?
