## Description

Aleksandra raised a real concern: some engineers (Steve explicitly) feel that AI adoption threatens their identity and sense of value. The fear isn't just about job security — it's about what it means to be good at your job when AI can do parts of it.

The "babysitter" framing (you're just supervising the AI) is being used dismissively internally, and it's landing badly. This needs proactive, honest conversations squad by squad — not a company-wide memo, but real 1:1 or small-group discussions.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Agree with Aleksandra on which squads / individuals most need this conversation
- [ ] Develop a framing that's honest (some roles will change) but grounding (the craft moves up, not away)
- [ ] Run conversations squad by squad — not a town hall
- [ ] Follow up: have the conversations shifted the mood?

## Notes

Aleksandra's read: the "understand vs trust" tension is at the heart of it. Engineers want to understand the code they ship. When an agent writes it, they feel detached from it. This is legitimate and worth engaging with — not dismissing.

Avoid: leaderboards, gamification, mandatory quotas. These will backfire with this cohort.
