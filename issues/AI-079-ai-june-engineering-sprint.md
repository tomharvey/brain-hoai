## Description

Fergus proposed "AI June" — a deliberate two-week period where the engineering team commits to trying AI-native ways of working, not as a side project but as a first-class investment. At the end, the team reviews: what worked, what didn't, what to invest in going forward.

The goal is to break apathy and create a shared reference point — rather than optional opt-in or mandated tool use, a bounded experiment that everyone participates in.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] AI June dates confirmed (targeting June 2026)
- [ ] Format defined: what does "doing AI June" mean for an engineer? (pairing, specific workflows, tooling experiments?)
- [ ] Communicated to engineering team with framing that makes it an investment, not a chore
- [ ] At least 2–3 specific pairing sessions run between Tom and individual engineers
- [ ] End-of-June retro: what worked, what didn't, what to standardise?

## Notes

Framing from Fergus: "stacking chips as a team" — the expectation is you engage, not that it's optional. But it doesn't need to be dictatorial.

Key concern: don't let it become about tooling for tooling's sake. The goal is for engineers to cross the threshold of finding AI genuinely useful in their actual day-to-day work — the "oh, this is a nice space" moment.

Pairing is probably the right mechanism. One-to-one, working on real tickets, showing AI-native approaches in context. The deliverable of the pairing session matters less than the exposure.

Watch for: Admiral visit (during June) may be a good moment to contrast approaches.
