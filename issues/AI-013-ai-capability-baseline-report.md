## Description

Produce a structured baseline assessment of AI capability across the company, drawn from the discovery round. This serves two purposes:

1. **Measurement**: a snapshot of where each department is today, so progress can be tracked over time
2. **Strategy**: the foundation for designing department-specific uplift paths — you can't design the programme without knowing the starting points

## Proposed structure

### Per department
- **Current AI usage**: what tools, how often, what for
- **Maturity level**: a simple scale (e.g. unaware → exploring → daily user → designing with AI → building with AI)
- **Key people**: power users, champions, blockers
- **Biggest gaps**: what they can't do yet but should be able to
- **Blockers**: tooling, data quality, confidence, time, culture
- **Recommended next step**: the single most impactful intervention for this team

### Cross-cutting
- Common blockers (HubSpot data quality, Retool dependencies, workshop scheduling)
- The capability spectrum: Fred → average user → Kirsty/Matt Lees
- What "good" looks like — Emily's ops team as reference model

## Data sources

Most of the raw data already exists in the vault:
- 20+ discovery meeting notes and transcripts
- People files with working style notes and AI usage observations
- Initiative files with per-team context
- WORKING.md cross-cutting themes

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Department-by-department capability snapshot covering Ops, Engineering, Product, Distribution, Finance, Underwriting
- [ ] Maturity ratings with evidence from discovery conversations
- [ ] Cross-cutting blockers identified and ranked
- [ ] Recommended interventions per department
- [ ] Working document format — primarily for Tom's use, informing the board roadmap (AI-001, due Apr 20)
