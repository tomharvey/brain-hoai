# AI-062 — Address AI job security concerns across engineering

## Context

Multiple engineers expressing anxiety about AI-driven redundancies, citing Facebook/FAANG layoffs as evidence. Alex (engineer) raised explicitly in a 1:1 with Tom on Friday 2026-05-22. Tom also heard similar concerns from someone else (name unresolved from transcript) on Wednesday.

Tom's counter-narrative: Facebook made redundancies for unrelated business reasons (aging user base, metaverse losses) — Zuckerberg needed a story for the board. Anthropic hiring engineers at $500k/year simultaneously contradicts the replacement narrative. Flock specifically is growing and hiring across all functions.

## Approach

- Mix of formats: targeted 1:1s for those who need personal conversation, curated group sessions for others
- Not random group allocation — Tom knows who needs which format
- Coverage across engineering + broader company where relevant
- Thread in activation pathway / new skills narrative as the positive frame

## Notes

The framing that lands: "engineers are x% more valuable now, which makes it x% easier to justify more of them — if the company has growth ambitions." Flock does. The failure mode of this argument is companies that have plateaued or are shrinking (the Facebook model). Flock is not that.
