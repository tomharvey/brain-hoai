Fergus identified two AI failure modes that should be built into all workshop framing:

1. **Over-engineering** — jumping straight to high-fidelity output without iterating through a conversation first
2. **Fire-and-forget** — sending a prompt and accepting whatever comes back without owning the output

## Action

- Reframe workshop content to explicitly address both failure modes
- Make "you own the output" a first principle in every session, not an afterthought
- Test whether the framing lands differently with technical vs. non-technical audiences

## Context

These failure modes are where AI adoption goes wrong quietly. People use the tool but produce worse outcomes than if they hadn't. The workshop is the intervention point.
