# Audience Guide: Broker

Use this file when the presentation audience is a **broker, MGA, or insurance intermediary**.

---

## Who This Is For

Brokers who place fleet insurance and are considering Flock as a market for their clients.
This includes: independent brokers, regional brokers, national brokers, and MGAs.

---

## What Brokers Care About

1. **Winning new fleet business** — they need a USP to differentiate vs competitors
2. **Retaining existing clients** — loss ratios, claims support, renewal competitiveness
3. **Ease of doing business** — fast quotes, clear wordings, responsive claims
4. **Data to demonstrate value to their clients** — something to show the fleet customer

---

## Tone

**Professional, partnership-focused, commercially sharp.**

- Treat the broker as a sophisticated commercial partner, not a sales prospect
- Lead with data and outcomes, not features
- Be direct about what Flock offers and what it doesn't
- Use "we" — position Flock as the broker's partner, not a competitor

---

---

## Key Messages (ensure these land)

1. **"We make your fleet clients safer — and that makes you look good."**
   - Flock gives brokers a differentiated proposition beyond just price
2. **"Our data is your data."**
   - Brokers get reporting they can use directly with their fleet clients
3. **"We reward the right risks."**
   - Safety rebates mean price-competitive renewal for well-managed fleets

---

## Data to Include (use real figures if provided; otherwise flag as illustrative)

- Fleet market claims inflation: ~15% YoY (industry benchmark — flag if using)
- Average Flock safety rebate: up to 10% of premium
- Flock book size: £40.8M GWP, 352 policies (can share with broker partners)
- Segments covered: Courier, Taxi, Self-Drive Hire, Trades

---

## Things to Watch Out For

- ⚠️ Do NOT share individual customer claims data with brokers — only aggregated/anonymised portfolio stats
- ⚠️ Do NOT make promises about specific pricing — all quotes are indicative
- ⚠️ Brokers are sophisticated — avoid condescending "insurance 101" content
- If the broker has specific clients they want to discuss, ask the user for those client details before building

---

## Slide Design Notes for Broker Decks

- Cover: include broker's name or logo context if known (ask user)
- Stats slides: use portfolio-level data — claims frequency, safety improvement, rebate stats
- The "partnership" frame works well on the cover section break — emphasise "together" language
- Close with a CTA slide that has 3 clear next steps (see brand.md CTA template)
