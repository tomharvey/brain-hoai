# Audience Guide: Board

Use this file when the presentation audience is a **board, executive team, or investor group**.

---

## Who This Is For

Senior internal stakeholders and external investors who need a high-level view of Flock's safety performance, strategic direction, or a specific initiative:
- **Flock board / executive team** — quarterly or annual performance review
- **Investors** — update on safety strategy, product progress, commercial impact
- **Leadership** — internal strategy or initiative proposal requiring sign-off

---

## What Board Audiences Care About

1. **Business performance vs targets** — are we on track? What's the trend?
2. **Strategic clarity** — what are we doing and why, in plain terms
3. **Risk** — what could go wrong, and what are we doing about it
4. **Financial impact** — what does this mean for GWP, loss ratio, revenue
5. **Decisions needed** — they want to leave knowing what was agreed

---

## Tone

**Executive, strategic, direct. No fluff.**

- Assume high intelligence and zero tolerance for waffle
- Lead with the "so what" — don't bury the conclusion
- Every slide should have one clear takeaway
- Use data to support narrative, not replace it
- Be willing to surface bad news — boards respect honesty

---

## Typical Deck Structure (8–12 slides)

| # | Slide Type | Title | Content |
|---|-----------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Cover | [TOPIC] — [QUARTER / DATE] | Meeting name, date, "CONFIDENTIAL \| FLOCKCOVER.COM" |
| 2 | Content | EXECUTIVE SUMMARY | 3–5 bullet points: the whole story in one slide — what's working, what's not, what's needed |
| 3 | Stats | HEADLINE PERFORMANCE | 3 hero stats most relevant to the audience: GWP, loss ratio, safety score trend |
| 4 | Content | PROGRESS AGAINST PLAN | RAG status on key initiatives — what's on track, at risk, behind |
| 5 | Stats | FINANCIAL IMPACT | Claims cost trend, rebate liability, premium earned — whichever is most relevant |
| 6 | Content | KEY RISKS | Top 3 risks with owners and mitigations — be direct |
| 7 | Content | STRATEGIC PRIORITIES | The 2–3 things that will move the needle — focus on outcomes not activities |
| 8 | Content | WHAT WE'RE ASKING FOR | Decisions, approvals, or awareness — be explicit about the ask |
| 9 | CTA | DECISIONS & NEXT STEPS | What was agreed, who owns what, by when |

---

## Key Messages (ensure these land)

1. **"Here's where we are — honestly."**
   - Boards respect transparency; don't oversell the positives
2. **"Safety improvement is a commercial lever, not just a product feature."**
   - Frame safety investment in terms of loss ratio, retention, and GWP
3. **"These are the decisions we need you to make."**
   - Always close with explicit asks — boards hate leaving without clarity

---

## Executive Summary Slide (critical — always include)

The executive summary should be the second slide and contain:
- **1 sentence**: What this presentation is about
- **2–3 bullets**: The key facts / performance summary
- **1 bullet**: The risks or challenges
- **1 bullet**: What we're asking for

Example structure:
```
This presentation covers Flock's Q1 2026 safety performance and the investment case for expanding our telemetry capabilities.

Performance:
• GWP: £40.8M (on track vs £42M target)
• Loss ratio: 68% (improved from 74% in Q4 2025)
• Safety score improvement: +4pts across portfolio

Challenge:
• Telematics data completeness below target in Taxi segment (82% vs 95% target)

Ask:
• Approve £150K investment in enhanced data pipeline to close the gap
```

---

## Data to Include

Ask the user for any of the following that are relevant:
- Current GWP and target
- Loss ratio (current and prior period)
- Portfolio safety score trend
- Claims frequency and severity trends
- Rebate liability (total value of rebates earned by customers)
- Segment breakdown (Courier 57%, Taxi 15%, SDH 15%, Trades 11%)
- Key initiative status (red/amber/green)

If data is not provided:
- ⚠️ Flag slides as "figures TBC — replace before presenting to board"
- Do NOT use placeholder numbers for financial metrics without clear labelling

---

## Slide Design Notes for Board Decks

- Every slide title should be the **conclusion**, not the topic:
  - ✅ "LOSS RATIO IMPROVED 6PP IN Q1" (not "LOSS RATIO UPDATE")
  - ✅ "TELEMATICS COVERAGE IS BELOW TARGET" (not "TELEMATICS STATUS")
- Stats slides: use 2 hero numbers maximum — boards don't need 3-stat grids
- Avoid bullet lists of more than 4 items — if you have 6 bullets, it's two slides
- RAG status tables: use `19A84F` green, `FF922E` orange, `F43D26` red for status — not the hero number colours
- The "ask" slide should be unmissable — large text, clear numbered items

---

## Things to Watch Out For

- ⚠️ Board decks must not contain commercially sensitive competitor data without permission
- ⚠️ If this is for an investor update, confirm with user whether it's a formal regulatory communication — tone and liability wording may need review
- ⚠️ Do NOT generate specific financial forecasts or projections without the user confirming the numbers — these have legal implications
- Always ask: "Is there anything that must not appear in this deck?" before building
