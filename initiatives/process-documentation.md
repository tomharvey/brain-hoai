## Summary

Before automating anything, the company needs to document what people actually do. Every discovery conversation this week reinforced this. The value isn't automation — it's codifying business logic. Once documented, processes can be improved or automated intelligently rather than replicating fragile workflows.

## Goal

Robust business process documentation across key operational areas — good enough that an agent could follow them, which means good enough that a human can too.

## Current state

- Underwriting assistance has a Notion doc that covers ~80% of the process — the missing 20% is institutional knowledge
- No systematic documentation across other departments
- "Load bearing Google sheets" exist but aren't mapped
- Chris: "some things we're automating shouldn't be that complicated in the first place"
- Fergus: third-party agent vendor was rejected because they'd just codify the existing broken process

## Dependencies

- Access to operational teams (Emily, Adam, finance)
- Discovery conversations to identify which processes matter most
- Willingness from teams to participate

## Risks

- Treated as a documentation exercise rather than a strategic one
- Teams too busy to engage
- Documentation becomes stale immediately

## Next actions

- [ ] Identify top 3 processes to document first (likely from Emily/Adam conversations)
- [ ] Explore PostHog/DOM log analysis as a shortcut (Matt/Geran idea)
- [ ] Use agent automation as the carrot — "teach the agent your job" frames it as empowerment not surveillance
