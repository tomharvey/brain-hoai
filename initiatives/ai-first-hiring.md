## Summary

Turn [[rob|Rob Grice]]'s self-started work on an AI-first engineering interview into a company standard: an interview where a candidate's AI practice IS the assessment surface, scored against the [[activation-pathway]] stages — then expand the format beyond engineering with [[eraaz-ali|Eraaz]] and the hiring managers.

Honours the existing owner: this is Rob's work. The initiative gives it structure, a live pilot, and a route out of engineering — not new ownership. (Confirm with Rob in London, w/c Jul 7, that he wants to own the engineering format as his named Q3 artifact.)

## Why

- Ed's hiring bar (Jun 24): "if an engineer says they use Copilot and have never used Claude or Cursor, don't hire them." A screening question is a weak test of that; watching someone work with AI is a strong one.
- The current format punishes the behaviour we hire for: a candidate was "caught using AI live during interview" this June and it counted against them ([[2026-06-11-tom-jordi-weekly]]). Inverting that norm is the whole idea.
- Every candidate exits with a provisional stage score → the capability baseline stops decaying as the company grows ([[AI-128]], [[AI-115]]).

## What changes under governance

- **Resource**: pilot slots on the live senior engineering lead search (two recruiters engaged since June); Tom's time for rubric-to-framework mapping.
- **Reporting**: progress via Tom/Jordi weeklies; expansion milestones via the People-team lane (Eraaz).
- **Alignment**: assessment rubric maps to [[activation-pathway]] stages; feeds new-hire baseline; consistent with the JD refresh ([[AI-155-jd-ai-content]]) so we assess what we advertise.

## What Rob needs

- Air cover to spend engineering time on a People-adjacent artifact (Jordi aware).
- A decision from Ed that this replaces the ad-hoc AI screening question for engineering hires (decision 5 in [[ed-meeting-prep-2026-07-08]]).

## Plan

1. **Document the format** (Rob, Q3): interview structure, task bank, scoring rubric mapped to stages 0–5. Output: a reusable artifact in the vault + hiring docs.
2. **Pilot on the live search** (Rob + hiring managers, this quarter): run on real senior-eng-lead candidates; calibrate Rob's stage-reads against hiring-manager reads.
3. **Port beyond engineering** (Eraaz + hiring managers): adapt per function — Eraaz already asks AI questions "in every single screening"; this gives them teeth. Start with the functions hiring next.
4. **Close the onboarding loop** (Eraaz + Tom): interview stage-read becomes the new joiner's baseline score; Kaylee-pattern starters (personal use, no work practice) spotted on day one.
