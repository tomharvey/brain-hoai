## Context and problem statement

The Pioneer board meeting on April 20th requires an updated roadmap view covering H2 2026 deliverables and a 24-month outlook. Fergus needs someone to own the AI portion. Should Tom take this on in his first two weeks?

## Decision drivers

- If Tom doesn't write the AI strategy, someone else will fill the vacuum at the board
- The roadmap is a forcing function for crystallising everything learned in discovery
- Draft due April 3rd gives Fergus 2+ weeks to refine before the board
- Tom has the most complete cross-company picture of AI efforts from discovery conversations

## Considered options

### Option 1 — Tom owns it

- Good, because it establishes authority and forces strategy to be written down
- Bad, because it's a lot to take on in week 2 of a new role

### Option 2 — Defer to Fergus or someone else

- Good, because less pressure on Tom
- Bad, because no one else has the cross-company AI picture; the roadmap would be weaker

## Decision outcome

**Chosen option:** Option 1 — Tom owns the AI section. Draft due end of Friday April 3rd. Format: now / next / later across 4 domains.

## Consequences

- AI-001 issue created to track this
- Discovery calls (Emily, Adam, Jordi) are inputs to the roadmap, not separate workstreams
- Fergus weekly on Thu Apr 2nd is the gut-check moment before polishing
- If the draft isn't ready by Friday, the board meeting prep is at risk
