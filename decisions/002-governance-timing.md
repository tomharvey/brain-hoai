## Context and problem statement

Sam raised that the company has no AI guardrails or governance framework. There are real risks (data exposure, prompt manipulation, regulatory). Should governance be a week-1 priority or deferred?

## Decision drivers

- The company is moving fast with AI adoption — "riding a power bike at 200 mph as an amateur"
- No incident has occurred yet, but the risk is real (regulated industry)
- Shipping tangible AI wins builds credibility for the new role
- Sam has PhD research capacity and is keen to contribute
- Paul (Head of Compliance) is the natural co-owner

## Considered options

### Option 1 — Start governance work now

- Good, because reduces risk exposure immediately
- Bad, because diverts energy from strategy and discovery in the critical first weeks of the role

### Option 2 — Defer to later Q2, track but don't push

- Good, because allows focus on board roadmap and tangible wins first
- Bad, because risk exposure continues; an incident before the framework exists would be painful

## Decision outcome

**Chosen option:** Option 2 — important but not urgent. Track it as an initiative, keep Sam warm, but don't invest energy until the board roadmap is shipped and initial wins are in motion.

## Consequences

- [[ai-governance-framework]] initiative created and tracked
- Sam knows he has a seat at the table when the work starts
- If an incident occurs before the framework exists, this decision will look wrong in hindsight — accepted risk
- Revisit timing after board meeting (late April)
