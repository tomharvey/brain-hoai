## Context and problem statement

Three independent groups are working on submissions automation (Abs/CC extraction, David, Chris + others). Should the Head of AI intervene now to consolidate, or finish the discovery round first?

## Decision drivers

- Tom is in his first week — doesn't yet have the full picture
- Abs is leaving in weeks — time pressure on handover
- Rushing to consolidate without understanding all efforts could backfire
- Need to understand distribution and ops perspectives before restructuring

## Considered options

### Option 1 — Intervene now

- Good, because stops further fragmentation immediately
- Bad, because Tom doesn't have enough context yet to make the right call on who leads or what the unified approach looks like

### Option 2 — Address holistically after discovery

- Good, because decisions will be better informed by conversations with Emily, Adam, and the wider company
- Bad, because fragmentation continues for another 1–2 weeks

## Decision outcome

**Chosen option:** Option 2 — address holistically after discovery round completes. The cost of another week of parallel work is lower than the cost of a premature consolidation that gets the architecture or ownership wrong.

## Consequences

- Abs handover must still happen regardless — tracked as [[cc-extraction-handover]]
- Consolidation conversation should happen by mid-April at the latest
- Discovery calls with Emily and Adam will surface the ops/distribution perspective on submissions
