# Workshop activity — design your first co:work project

You and your pair are each going to design a co:work project. Claude will coach you through it.

**What you'll have at the end:** A clear intent, a context list, and a version zero — ready to build from.

---

## Step 1 — Setup (2 min)

1. Download `cowork-coach.md` from the Slack thread
2. Create a new folder on your Desktop — name it after yourself, e.g. `sarah-cowork`
3. Move `cowork-coach.md` into that folder
4. Open **Claude Desktop** and click **Projects** in the left sidebar, then **+ New project**

You'll be asked for a few things — use these exactly:

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Name** | Your first name + "co:work" — e.g. `Sarah co:work` |
| **Description** | `You are a co:work project coach. Read cowork-coach.md and follow its instructions exactly. Do not do anything else until you have read that file.` |
| **Add content** | Click **Add files** and select `cowork-coach.md` from your Desktop folder |

Click **Create project**.

Then open the project and set the model to **Claude Opus 4.8**.

Then leave it — don't start a conversation yet.

---

## Step 2 — Write your rough answers (10 min)

**Person A goes first (5 min).** Person A answers the questions out loud. Person B listens and helps — ask "what do you mean by that?", push back on anything that sounds like two intents, question whether the version zero is really the smallest thing. Person A writes down what lands.

**Then swap (5 min).** Person B answers, Person A helps.

Don't write for each other — help each other think.

**First: what type of work is this?**

- **Task-based** — the same thing comes around repeatedly, with a clear start and end each time *(like Shreya's NOC letters)*
- **Relationship-based** — ongoing context with a person or group that gets richer over time *(like Adam's broker briefings)*
- **Something else** — describe it in your own words

**1. What's your intent?**
Finish this sentence: *"I want to ___"*
One sentence. If it contains "and", pick one half and write the other one down separately.

**2. What context does the AI need?**
Imagine briefing a smart new colleague who knows nothing about your work. What would they need to know?
Think about: relevant files or data, how things work in your team, who the key people are, what a good result looks like.

**3. What's your version zero?**
Not a dashboard. The single conversation you'd have with the AI first — if you were doing this manually, right now.
If it has multiple parts, write just the first one.

**4. Sanity check**
Is this one intent or three? If it's more than one, pick the most useful one for this week. Write the others down — they become the next projects.

## Want to go further? Three ways to give Claude richer context

These are optional. Try one if you want — they take a minute to set up but give Claude something more to work with than typed notes.

**Draw it** *(no setup)*
Sketch your workflow, your team structure, or your process on paper or a whiteboard. Take a photo. Drag the image straight into Claude Desktop. Claude can read it.
*Best for: anyone who thinks visually, or whose process is hard to describe in words.*

**Use a Notion page** *(no setup — Notion MCP already connected)*
If the context you need already lives in a Notion page — a process doc, a project brief, a team dashboard — don't retype it. Just tell Claude:
> *"I have a Notion page called [name] — can you read it and use it as context?"*
Claude will pull it in directly. If Notion MCP isn't responding, skip this and type instead.
*Best for: anyone whose work is already documented in Notion.*

**Speak your answers** *(30 sec setup)*
If you find it easier to talk than type, enable Mac dictation: **System Settings → Keyboard → Dictation → On**. Then press the microphone key twice to start speaking into any text field.
Note: works better in quiet spaces — remote participants in breakout rooms will find this easier than in-person.
*Best for: anyone who's faster talking than typing, or who wants to think out loud.*

## Step 3 — Co:work coaching (15 min)

**Both open co:work at the same time.** Each person works through their own coaching conversation independently — but stay in contact with your pair as you go.

To start, type:

> *"I've been thinking about a co:work project. Here are my rough answers to the intake questions:"*

Then type or paste what you wrote in Step 2.

Claude will ask you questions one at a time. Answer honestly. Share what it's asking you with your pair — compare what it's pushing back on. If your pair got a harder question than you, ask yourself why.

**Claude will end by** setting a scheduled check-in, writing your brief to `my-brief.md`, and giving you a closing summary. Copy the summary — you'll share it in the debrief.

---

*If you finish early: help your pair work through theirs.*

---

## Why this is a project, not a chat

Everything you told Claude in the last 15 minutes is saved in this project. Your intent, your context, your version zero, your commitment — all of it is in `my-brief.md` in your project folder.

At the end of the coaching session, Claude will set a scheduled check-in directly in the conversation — no extra steps needed.

When you're ready to build, open the project, start a new task, and type:

> *"The coaching is complete. Read my-brief.md and help me start building."*

Claude will pick up from exactly where you left off. A chat window would have forgotten everything.
