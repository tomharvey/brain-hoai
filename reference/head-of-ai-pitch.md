# Head of AI at Flock

---

## The opportunity, right now

AI has been picked up across the company with enthusiasm and Flock is well recognised for the innovation.

We've got momentum, but without someone pulling us together, the deliverables will drift, work will overlap, and the moment could be lost. There are strong examples where the company has come together around AI, but also where a lack of focus has left an opportunity to stagnate.

---

## Current initiatives

In the course of the Head of Engineering role, I've carved out time to kick-start, continue or just deliver some AI initiatives.

### MCP server

I kick-started the MCP server work with a proof of concept. The most basic version opened up product innovation and brought faster iteration to the teams.
Huge credit to the teams in picking that up and running with it. It now needs a framework for feedback and a vision for where it goes.

### Agent workshop

Taking the team through the basics of building an Agent demystified this new type of software and gave many in the team the confidence to
explore innovative product types.

### Internal frameworks

In the past 2 weeks I've delivered a technical framework for monitoring the token cost and a framework for deploying Agents as core parts of the
platform-services. This is to move us away from technical proofs-of-concept towards treating them as core, productionised and tracked assets.

### Personal projects — proof of end-to-end AI product thinking

In the evenings and weekends, I've been building AI-first products as an AI-native developer. Including a natural language querying layer over a real-time data platform — which has given me a different kind of depth than you get from integrating AI into existing products. I know what the hard problems actually are and how
we move from an AI toy to something that uses real customer feedback to build an innovation moat.

### Summary

These initiatives have build skills and energy but the learning isn't being shared. They've delivered tangible results already but the capabilities aren't flowing into the core product.

## What the role would actually involve

### Days 1–30 — Foundations

Laying the foundation. Shipping real work and using it to define opportunity, frameworks and strategy.

- Assessment of team AI capability and what would move the needle.
- Shortlist of AI opportunities with product and within business processes — ranked by impact and feasibility.
- Development of core AI framework by delivering productionised features.

### Days 31–60 — Delivery

Demonstrate an increased pace of delivery.

- The capability gaps identified in month one are closing as the team ships and uses AI with confidence.
- A repeatable process for closing product gaps. Demonstrable gaps identified with data and solved with AI.
- Core business processes mapped so AI can automate and highlight the gaps.
- Create a base-layer of insight across multiple domains; each trip, each claim, each quote.

### Days 61–90 — Momentum

New concepts are embedded and natural ways of working.

- AI capability is high across the team, and growing independently of a dedicated Head of AI.
- Using AI to shorten the iteration loop is a natural way of working.
- Business processes are accessible and automated by AI. Clear roadmap for how and when to automate more.
- We treat **insight** as naturally as we treat **telemetry data**. We're using pipelines to aggregate insight and customers are acting on it.

### Ongoing success

Success is that everyone is their own Head of AI. Each person is capable of growing their own capabilities and the team is working as one with the new technology.

- Prodtech team deliver agentic systems as naturally as non-agentic. They prefer the iteration speed and insight agentic services deliver.
- Business processes are better understood, AI-accessible. The definition of each process is core to its operation.
- We know where the biggest gains come from and how to unlock them. Our new frameworks allow us to move fast.
- Innovation is a visible differentiator — for hiring, partnerships, and reputation.

I'll work closely with Jordi and Matt on how we create capabilities in Prodtech, and with a small team across the business to deliver these changes to business processes.
